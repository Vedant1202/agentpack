The pension accumulated benefit obligation represents the actuarial present value of benefits based on employee service and compensation as of the
measurement date and does not include an assumption about future compensation levels. The accumulated benefit obligation of the U.S. pension plans was
$15.033 billion and $16.270 billion at December 31, 2018 and 2017, respectively. The accumulated benefit obligation of the international pension plans
was $6.438 billion and $6.870 billion at December 31, 2018 and 2017, respectively.
