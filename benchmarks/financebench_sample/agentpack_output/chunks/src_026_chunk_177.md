3.    COMMON STOCK IN A U.S. CORPORATION
Shares of common stock of a U.S. corporation are analogous to ordinary shares of an Australian corporation. Each holder of a Share is
entitled to one vote for every Share held. Dividends may be paid on the Shares out of any funds of the Company legally available for
dividends at the discretion of the Board. The Shares are traded on the NYSE in the United States of America and are traded under the
symbol “MMM.” The Shares are not liable to any further calls for payment of capital or for other assessment by the Company and have no
sinking fund provisions, pre-emptive rights, conversion rights or redemption provisions.
4.    ASCERTAINING THE MARKET PRICE OF SHARES
Participant may ascertain the current or historical market price of the Shares as traded on the NYSE at http://www.nyse.com under the
symbol “MMM.” The AUD equivalent of that price can be obtained at: http://www.rba.gov.au/statistics/frequency/exchange-rates.html.
5.    GRANT AND EXERCISE PRICE OF THE OPTION
Participant is not required to pay any money to receive the Option. However, as explained below, Participant must pay an exercise price in
order to receive the underlying Shares.
The exercise price (i.e., the price Participant must pay to acquire shares on the exercise of an Option) is determined by the Company,
provided, that such exercise price shall not be less than the Fair Market Value of a Share on the date of grant of such Option.
The exercise price is denominated in U.S. dollars and must be paid in U.S. dollars. The Australian dollar amount required to exercise the
Option and acquire Shares will be that amount which, when converted into U.S. dollars on the date of exercise, equals the exercise price,
denominated in U.S. dollars. The Australian dollar equivalent of the exercise price will change with fluctuations in the U.S./Australian dollar
exchange rate.
