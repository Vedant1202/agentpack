Assumptions
Accounting for pensions and other postretirement benefits requires an extensive use of assumptions about the discount rate, expected return on plan
assets, the rate of future compensation increases received by the Company’s employees, mortality, turnover and medical costs. Each assumption is reviewed
annually. The assumptions are selected to represent the average expected experience over time and may differ in any one year from actual experience due to
changes in capital markets and the overall economy. These differences will impact the amount of pension and other postretirement benefit expense that the
Company recognizes.

The discount rate assumption was determined for the pension and postretirement benefit plans independently. The Company uses an approach that
approximates the process of settlement of obligations tailored to the plans’ expected cash flows by matching the plans’ cash flows to the coupons and expected
maturity values of individually selected bonds. Historically, for each plan, the discount rate was developed at the level equivalent rate that would produce the
same present value as that using spot rates aligned with the projected benefit payments.
