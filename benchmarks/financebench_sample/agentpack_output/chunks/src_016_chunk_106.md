Adjusted net income decreased $260 million, primarily due to lower margins at our South America and US and Utilities SBUs and an
increase in interest expense, partially offset by higher margins at our MCAC and Eurasia SBUs and an increase in interest income.
•
Working capital requirements decreased $1.1 billion, primarily due to deferred income at Angamos in the 2021 due to revenue
recognized for the early contract terminations with Minera Escondida and Minera Spence, the GSF liability payment at Tietê in 2021,
and the change in income tax liabilities, partially offset by an increase in inventory, primarily fuel and other raw materials, at AES Andes,
AES Panama, and AES Indiana.
