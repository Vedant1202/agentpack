Sale of New York American Water Company, Inc.
On January 1, 2022, the Company completed the previously disclosed sale of its regulated utility operations in New York to Liberty Utilities (Eastern
Water Holdings) Corp. (“Liberty”), an indirect, wholly owned subsidiary of Algonquin Power & Utilities Corp. Liberty purchased from the Company all of the
capital stock of the Company’s New York subsidiary for a purchase price of $608 million in cash. See Note 5—Acquisitions and Divestitures in the Notes to
Consolidated Financial Statements for additional information.
Sale of Michigan American Water Company
On February 4, 2022, the Company completed the sale of its operations in Michigan for $6 million in cash.
Future Growth
The Company expects to invest between $14 billion to $15 billion over the next five years, and between $30 billion to $34 billion over the next 10 years,
including $2.9 billion in 2023. The Company’s expected future investments include:
•
capital investment for infrastructure improvements in the Regulated Businesses between $12.5 billion to $13 billion over the next five years, and
between $27 billion to $30 billion over the next 10 years, including $2.5 billion expected in 2023; and
•
growth from acquisitions in the Regulated Businesses to expand the Company’s water and wastewater customer base of between $1.5 billion to
$2 billion over the next five years, and between $3 billion to $4 billion over the next 10 years, including $400 million expected in 2023.
